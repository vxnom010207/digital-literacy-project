# UPI Fraud Case Study

UPI fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, especially among students and young users. In this type of fraud, scammers trick users into sharing sensitive information or approving fake payment requests. A typical scenario involves a fraudster sending a message or calling the victim, pretending to be from a bank or a trusted service. They may claim that the user has won a reward, needs to receive a refund, or must verify their account.

The attacker then sends a payment request or a QR code and convinces the victim to approve it. Many users mistakenly believe that approving the request will help them receive money, but instead, money is deducted from their account. In some cases, users are also asked to share OTPs or PINs, which leads to further financial loss.

Students are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of such scams. The consequences include loss of money, stress, and compromise of personal information. With the increasing use of digital payments, such frauds are becoming more frequent.

This case highlights the importance of awareness and careful handling of online transactions to avoid becoming a victim of cybercrime.
